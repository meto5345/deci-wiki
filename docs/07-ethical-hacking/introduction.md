# Introduction to Ethical Hacking

**TL;DR:** Ethical hackers are good guys hired to find security flaws before bad guys do.

:::info Course Context
* Course: Level 3 Cybersecurity
* Module: Week 7 — Ethical Hacking
* Topic: Topic 1
:::

## Step 1: The Golden Rule (Get Permission)

* **No permission = Illegal.** Intentions do not matter.
* **The Rules:** You must have written papers called a **Scope of Work (SoW)** and **Rules of Engagement (RoE)** before touching any network.
* **The Laws:** Unauthorized access is punished by the **CFAA** (US law) and the **CMA** (UK law).

## Step 2: Hacker Types

* **White Hat:** Good guys. They have **full permission** and fix gaps.
* **Black Hat:** Bad guys. They have **zero permission** and steal data.
* **Grey Hat:** In-between. They hack **without permission** but don't steal. Legally risky.

## Step 3: The 5 Hacking Steps

1. **Reconnaissance:** Look at the target from far away.
2. **Scanning:** Check doors and windows up close.
3. **Exploitation:** Safely step inside a weak door.
4. **Post-Exploitation:** See what valuable files are inside.
5. **Reporting:** Write a guide explaining how to lock it all up.

## Step 4: Real-World Breakdown

1. **Look:** Find out who owns a domain name.
2. **Check:** Find an open network doorway called **Port 22** (used for remote logins).
3. **Analyze:** Discover the login software is old and has a public bug (**CVE-2023-38408**).
4. **Fix:** Tell the owner to update their software immediately.

## Quick Definitions

* **Ethical Hacking:** Finding software flaws with permission to fix them.
* **Penetration Testing:** A simulated attack to test defenses.
* **CVE:** A public dictionary list tracking known software bugs.
* **Scope:** The strict boundaries defining what you are allowed to test.
